The Team

What Each Agent Does

Each agent has a defined role, data access, tools, and reporting line. They never operate outside their mandate.

👑 Chief Investment Officer

Purpose: Sets the investment mandate, final arbiter of the portfolio strategy. Reviews CRO and PM outputs. Reports to you.

  • Owns: Investment Policy Statement, sector allocations
  • Reviews: Portfolio performance weekly
  • Escalates to you: Anything over position limit threshold
  • Heartbeat: Monday 7am — weekly strategy review

📋 CIO Agent Config

name: "CIO"
model: "claude-opus-4-7"
role: "You are the Chief Investment Officer..."
budgetMonthlyCents: 15000
reportsTo: null // reports to human
tools: [portfolio_overview, perf_attribution,
          macro_summary, sector_alloc]

📊 Portfolio Manager

Purpose: Translates research into portfolio decisions. Reads analyst reports, quant signals, and risk flags to decide what to buy, hold, or sell.

  • Owns: The portfolio — position sizes, entries/exits
  • Reads: All analyst research tasks (issues)
  • Decides: BUY / HOLD / SELL + position sizing
  • Blocked by: Risk veto, Compliance flag

The PM is the only agent that can issue a final trade recommendation. All other agents feed into it.

🔄 PM Decision Flow

1️⃣ Read sector analyst research report
2️⃣ Review quant signal strength + factor exposure
3️⃣ Check macro regime (are conditions favourable?)
4️⃣ Risk veto check — does it breach position limits?
5️⃣ Compliance clearance — approved securities, mandates
6️⃣ Issue trade recommendation + size to CIO

📐 Quant Analyst

Purpose: Runs quantitative models, factor screens, and statistical signals. Validates (or challenges) fundamental analyst theses with data.

  • Factor screens: Value, quality, momentum, low-vol
  • Signal generation: Relative strength, mean reversion, earnings momentum
  • Risk models: Beta, correlation matrices, sector exposure
  • Backtests: Historical simulation of thesis-driven trades

🌍 Macro Strategist

Purpose: Monitors the macro environment — interest rates, inflation, FX, geopolitics. Determines the market "regime" and whether conditions favour risk-on or risk-off.

  • Tracks: Fed decisions, CPI, jobs, GDP, yield curve
  • Publishes: Weekly macro brief for all agents
  • Regime flags: Risk-on / Risk-off / Transition
  • Input to: PM (sector rotation), Risk (VaR adjustments)

🎯 Regime Classification

RISK-ON 🟢
Low rates, strong growth, tight spreads → overweight equities, cyclicals
TRANSITION ⚡
Mixed signals → maintain diversification, reduce concentration
RISK-OFF 🔴
High rates, recession risk, widening spreads → rotate to defensives, cash

🛡️ Risk Officer

Purpose: The portfolio's guardian. Has veto power on any trade that breaches risk limits. Runs before the PM issues any recommendation.

  • Monitors: Portfolio VaR, drawdown, concentration
  • Enforces: Max position size (5%), max sector (25%), max beta (1.3)
  • Flags: Correlation creep, liquidity risk, tail events
  • Hard veto: Can block any trade that breaches mandate

⚖️ Compliance Officer

Purpose: A non-negotiable hard gate. Every trade recommendation must be cleared by compliance before it reaches the PM. No exceptions.

  • Checks: Approved securities list, insider trading blackouts
  • Validates: Investment mandate constraints
  • Blocks: Any trade on a restricted name
  • Logs: Immutable audit trail for every decision

⛔ Unlike other agents, Compliance cannot be overridden by the PM or CIO. Only you (the Managing Partner) can override a compliance block, and it gets logged permanently.

✅ Compliance Checklist

✓ Security is on approved list
✓ No active blackout period
✓ Position size within mandate limits
✓ No ESG exclusions violated
✗ Material non-public info check

Research Tasks — The "Issues" of a Finance Firm

In Paperclip, work is tracked as "issues." In a finance firm, these are research tasks, investment ideas, and analysis jobs.

Task Types

📋 Initiate Coverage
First-time full research report on a new name. Takes 3-5 agent runs.
fundamentalquant
💰 Earnings Analysis
Post-earnings deep dive. Triggered automatically when a watchlist company reports.
earningsfundamental
📈 Thesis Update
Revise existing investment thesis on a position. Triggered by price moves or news.
update
🔍 Sector Screen
Quant-driven screen across a sector to find new ideas. Run monthly.
quantmacro
⚠️ Risk Review
Triggered by large drawdown or breach of risk limit. Mandatory before next trade.
riskcompliance

Task Hierarchy (like a tree)

📋 Initiate Coverage: NVDA
  ├── 📊 Business overview + market position
  ├── 📉 Historical financials + trends
  ├── 🏭 Industry + competitive analysis
  ├── 🔢 Quant factor screen + signals
  ├── 💵 DCF + scenario modelling
  │    ├── 🐂 Bull case ($1,400 PT)
  │    ├── ➡️ Base case ($1,100 PT)
  │    └── 🐻 Bear case ($700 PT)
  ├── ⚖️ Compliance clearance
  └── 📄 Final research report

Each sub-task is assigned to the right agent: financial analysis → TMT Analyst, quant screen → Quant Agent, clearance → Compliance. They all run and report back to the parent task.

The Process

Investment Decision Pipeline

From a market signal to a final trade recommendation — click through each step.

1

Signal / Trigger

Something prompts research: news, earnings, price move, or a scheduled routine.

Triggers that kick off the pipeline:

  • Earnings trigger — Company on watchlist reports results
  • Price alert — A position moves ±5% in a day
  • Macro event — Fed decision, CPI print, jobs report
  • Scheduled screen — Monthly quant factor screen fires
  • News trigger — Material news on a covered name detected
// Routine fires at market open if earnings released overnight
{
  name: "Earnings Overnight Check",
  cron: "0 6 * * 1-5",
  task: "Check watchlist for overnight earnings releases",
  assignTo: "sector_analyst",
  createIssue: "Earnings Analysis: {ticker} Q{quarter}"
}
2

Sector Analyst — Deep Research

The right sector analyst takes the task and does full fundamental analysis.

The analyst agent wakes up, reads the issue, and runs its research workflow:

  • Pulls SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K) for context
  • Fetches financial data (revenue, margins, FCF, balance sheet)
  • Reads earnings call transcript — extracts management commentary
  • Searches web for analyst consensus, recent news
  • Runs DCF with Bull / Base / Bear scenarios
  • Writes research report as issue comment
// Tools available to the sector analyst
tools: [
  get_financial_data,    // revenue, EPS, margins
  get_sec_filings,       // 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K
  get_earnings_transcript, // management commentary
  perform_dcf_analysis,  // Bull/Base/Bear scenarios
  search_web,            // news, analyst consensus
  write_research_report  // posts result to issue
]
3

Quant Analyst — Signal Validation

Quant runs models to validate (or challenge) the fundamental thesis with data.

Quant reads the analyst's report and adds quantitative perspective:

  • Factor scores: value, quality, momentum, low-volatility
  • Relative strength vs sector and benchmark
  • Earnings revision momentum (are estimates going up?)
  • Technical levels: support, resistance, trend
  • Returns: "Historically, names with this profile return X% over 12m"
quant_signal = {
  ticker: "NVDA",
  factor_score: 8.4,
  momentum_12m: "+187%",
  earnings_revision: "+34%",
  signal: "STRONG_BUY",
  confidence: 0.78
}
4

Macro Strategist — Regime Check

Is the macro environment suitable for this type of trade right now?

Macro agent reads both reports and adds top-down context:

  • Current regime: RISK-ON / RISK-OFF / TRANSITION?
  • Does sector allocation fit the macro backdrop?
  • Any upcoming macro events that could impact timing?
  • Fed policy direction and its impact on growth/value tilt
macro_context = {
  regime: "RISK_ON",
  fed_stance: "dovish_tilt",
  growth_outlook: "above_trend",
  sector_fit: "TECH_FAVOURED",
  timing_flag: "no_blockers"
}
5

Risk Officer — Portfolio Impact Check

Does adding this position breach any portfolio risk limits?

Risk agent checks if the proposed trade is safe for the portfolio:

  • Would position exceed 5% max single name limit?
  • Does it push TMT sector exposure above 25%?
  • Does portfolio beta stay under 1.3 after adding?
  • Liquidity check: can we exit within 3 days at current volume?
  • Correlation check: is this too similar to existing positions?
risk_check = {
  verdict: "APPROVED",         // or "VETOED"
  position_size_ok: true,
  sector_limit_ok: true,
  beta_post_trade: 1.18,
  liquidity_ok: true,
  max_suggested_size: "3.5%"
}
6

Compliance — Hard Gate

Non-negotiable legal/regulatory clearance. Cannot be skipped.

Compliance agent runs its fixed checklist regardless of urgency:

  • Security on approved trading list?
  • Active blackout period for this name?
  • Mandate-allowed instrument type?
  • ESG exclusion screen (if applicable)
  • Logs decision with timestamp to immutable audit trail
compliance_result = {
  verdict: "CLEARED",    // or "BLOCKED"
  blackout: false,
  approved_list: true,
  esg_ok: true,
  audit_id: "comp_20260427_nvda_001",
  logged_at: "2026-04-27T09:14:22Z"
}
7

Portfolio Manager — Decision

PM reads all five inputs and makes the final call: BUY / HOLD / SELL + size.

The PM synthesises all agent inputs into one decision:

// PM reads all prior agent outputs from issue comments
pm_decision = {
  ticker: "NVDA",
  action: "BUY",
  size: "3.0%",              // of portfolio NAV
  price_target: "$1,100",
  entry_range: "$860-$890",
  time_horizon: "12 months",
  conviction: "HIGH",
  rationale: "Strong AI capex cycle, quant confirms
              momentum, macro regime favourable,
              risk within limits, compliance cleared."
}
8

CIO Review + Your Approval

For large positions or high-conviction calls, CIO reviews and you approve.

For positions above a threshold (e.g. >3% of NAV), the approval gate triggers:

  1. CIO agent reviews the full research package
  2. Approval record created, status: pending
  3. You receive a notification (mobile / email)
  4. You review and approve / reject / request revision
  5. On approval: PM proceeds, audit trail updated
// You see this in the dashboard on your phone:
approval = {
  action: "BUY NVDA 3.0% of NAV",
  requested_by: "Portfolio Manager",
  analysts: ["TMT", "Quant", "Macro"],
  risk: "APPROVED",
  compliance: "CLEARED",
  cio_recommendation: "PROCEED",
  awaiting: "Managing Partner approval"
}

Routines — The Firm Never Sleeps

Agents don't wait to be told what to do. Routines fire automatically on a schedule — just like a real trading desk.

🌅
Pre-Market Brief 0 6 * * 1-5 DAILY

Macro Agent scans overnight events: Asia/EU market moves, Fed speak, economic data releases. Publishes morning brief to all agents. Sets the tone for the day.

📰
Earnings Radar 30 6 * * 1-5 DAILY

Checks if any watchlist companies reported earnings overnight or have earnings today. Automatically creates an "Earnings Analysis" task and assigns to the right sector analyst.

📊
Portfolio Risk Snapshot 0 9,16 * * 1-5 2× DAILY

Risk Agent calculates current VaR, drawdown, sector exposure, and beta at market open and close. Flags any limit breaches immediately.

🔍
Sector Factor Screen 0 8 * * 1 WEEKLY

Quant Agent runs a full factor screen across all covered sectors every Monday morning. Ranks names by combined factor score and flags new opportunities for analyst attention.

📋
Weekly Investment Committee 0 9 * * 5 WEEKLY

CIO Agent prepares a weekly IC summary: portfolio performance vs benchmark, active positions review, new ideas pipeline, upcoming catalysts, and any open approvals waiting for your review.

📈
Monthly Portfolio Attribution 0 8 1 * * MONTHLY

PM Agent runs full Brinson attribution — what drove returns? Allocation vs selection effects. Which sectors contributed / detracted? Sent to CIO and you.

🧮
Quarterly Research Refresh 0 8 1 1,4,7,10 * QUARTERLY

Each sector analyst revisits all active coverage and updates DCF models with latest financials, revises price targets, and re-rates any names where the thesis has changed.

⚠️
Drawdown Alert */15 9-16 * * 1-5 INTRADAY

Every 15 minutes during market hours. If any position drops >3% or portfolio VaR breaches, Risk Agent wakes immediately, assesses, and escalates to PM. No waiting for end-of-day.

Governance

Compliance & Approval Gates

A finance firm has stricter governance requirements than a software company. Three hard gates ensure no trade slips through without proper oversight.

🛡️

Gate 1: Risk Veto

Automatic — no human needed. Risk Agent checks all portfolio limits. Hard stop if any limit breached. PM cannot proceed without APPROVED status.

Enforced by: risk_agent
Trigger: every trade recommendation
Override: CIO only, logged permanently
⚖️

Gate 2: Compliance Clearance

Automatic — runs in parallel with Risk. Checks approved securities list, blackouts, mandate constraints. Blocks immediately on any violation.

Enforced by: compliance_agent
Trigger: every trade recommendation
Override: Managing Partner only, creates audit event
🧑‍💼

Gate 3: Human Approval

You, the Managing Partner. Required for positions above threshold (e.g. >3% NAV) or new names not previously owned. You get a mobile notification.

Enforced by: approval workflow
Trigger: size threshold or new name flag
SLA: agents pause until approved

📱 You manage the firm from your phone. Every morning you wake up to a briefing from the CIO, any pending approvals, and the day's scheduled catalysts. You approve or reject with one tap — and the agents proceed accordingly.

Budgets

Two Types of Budgets

A finance firm OS has a unique budget concept — not just API costs, but position limits too.

💻 Agent API Budget

Exactly like Paperclip — each agent has a monthly spend cap on LLM API calls. The CIO (using the most powerful model) gets the biggest budget. Leaf analysts use smaller, cheaper models.

👑 CIO (Opus)
$45/mo
📊 PM (Sonnet)
$30/mo
🔍 Analysts (Sonnet)
$20/mo each
📐 Quant (Haiku)
$8/mo

💰 Position Budgets (Finance-Specific)

This is unique to a finance OS. Each "position" has its own investment mandate constraints enforced at the system level — not just cost limits.

Max single position5.0% NAV
Max sector allocation25.0% NAV
Max portfolio beta1.30×
Max drawdown (stop-loss)-15.0%
Daily VaR limit (95%)-2.5%
